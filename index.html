Real-time Co-Authoring

Multiple architects and engineers edit the same Revit model simultaneously from anywhere. Changes sync live — no emailing files back and forth, no arguments about who has the "latest version."

Design Collaboration & Package Sharing

Teams publish packages of work-in-progress design data on a timeline. See who shared what and when, compare versions visually, and consume updates only when you're ready — so downstream disciplines never get blindsided mid-task.

Model Coordination & Clash Detection

Automatic clash detection across architectural, structural, and MEP models. The system flags conflicts — like a duct running through a beam — before they become expensive on-site rework. Powered by Navisworks integration.

Issue Tracking & Markups

Create, assign, and resolve issues anchored to 3D elements or 2D sheets. Each carries a status workflow (Open → In Review → Resolved), assignees, due dates, photos, and markups — syncing bidirectionally between Revit, Navisworks, and web/mobile.

Dashboards & Analytics

Project-level dashboards showing open issues, overdue items, design risks, clash trends, and team activity — so BIM managers and project leads can spot bottlenecks early and act on them.

Frequently asked questions.

What is a CDE and why do I need one?
A Common Data Environment is the single, controlled source of truth for project information — drawings, models, correspondence, approvals. It replaces email chains and shared drives with structured workflows, audit trails, and enforced review gates. For any project of meaningful size, a CDE isn't optional; it's the only way coordination holds together.
Is JKR BINAXONE compliant with ISO 19650 and JKR BIM standards?
Yes. The platform is built around ISO 19650 information states and JKR BIM standard deliverables, including BPEP structure, naming conventions, and transmittal workflows. Reviews, approvals, and digital signatures are captured in a form that maps directly to JKR audit expectations.
Where is my data stored?
On TM One Cloud Alpha, hosted in Cyberjaya, Malaysia — a Tier III data centre operated by a Malaysian vendor. Your project data is stored, processed, and backed up entirely within Malaysian jurisdiction.
What file formats are supported?
JKR BINAXONE is IFC-native and supports common open BIM formats, alongside standard document formats (PDF, DWG, DOCX, XLSX, images). Open formats are first-class citizens — not a second-tier export path.
How does JKR BINAXONE compare to foreign CDE platforms?
Foreign CDE platforms weren't designed around JKR workflows, Malaysian procurement, or local data-sovereignty requirements. We are. You get equivalent or better technical capability, with hosting, support, and accountability inside the country — at licensing structures that match local procurement practice.
Can JKR BINAXONE be deployed on-premise?
Yes. Enterprise engagements support dedicated tenancy on TM One Cloud Alpha, hybrid deployments, or full on-premise installation inside an agency's own data centre for the most sensitive programmes.
What does "post-quantum cryptography" mean for my documents?
Today's digital signatures can become forgeable once quantum computing matures. Infrastructure projects outlive the cryptography that signed them. Post-quantum algorithms are resistant to attacks from future quantum machines, which means a document signed in JKR BINAXONE today stays legally and technically verifiable decades from now — aligned with Malaysian national cybersecurity direction.
How is JKR BINAXONE priced?
Three tiers — Starter, Professional, Enterprise — each quoted to scope. Licensing can be structured as royalty-based, annual subscription, or pay-per-project to match government procurement processes and private-sector contracts. Contact sales for a tailored proposal.
